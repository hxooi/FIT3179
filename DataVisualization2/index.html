UNICORN STARTUPS

According to Wikipedia, privately held startup companies that are valued at over US$1 billion are known as unicorns. The term unicorn was first coined in 2013, and uses the name of a mythical animal to represent the rarity of such success. In this visualization, let us explore the world of unicorns and analyze their success from different perspectives!

Unicorn Startups around the World

The map shows where unicorn startups come from. The United States has the most, followed by China and India.

The United States leads because it has many investors, strong startup culture, and big tech companies that support new ideas. With access to funding and global markets, it becomes easier for American startups to grow into unicorns.

Investors of Unicorns and their Investment Breakdown

Investors play an important role in the growth of unicorn companies. The charts below show how top investors distribute their funding — both by country and by industry. We can see clear differences in focus areas and investment strategies.

Most unicorn investments come from the United States and China, showing their strong dominance in global startup funding. This suggests that investors concentrate heavily in regions with well-developed tech ecosystems.

Fintech, AI, and E-commerce receive the largest share of investments, indicating investor confidence in these fast-growing sectors. These industries are seen as having the highest potential for innovation and long-term returns.
